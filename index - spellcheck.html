Summary/Abstract

Welcome to the Robotics Challenge. The purpose of this project is to build an autonomous vehicle (robot) that is mechanically sound to traverse rough terrain and smart enough to compute obstacle avoidance. All of this is done to navigate towards a beacon emitting a frequency which it can detect.

Project Background

With technological advances as a society we still have a myriad of obstacles in interplanetary exploration. The most feasible idea is having an autonomous robot to obtain and analyze samples in place of a manned flight. Communications between the earth and an interplanetary explorer have significant delays, thus creating the need for autonomous controls and decision making in the exploration vehicle itself. Due to NASA's involvement with this open competitions it encourages development of new designs and ideas that can eventually be used to make better and more efficient designs for the possibility of future use.

Constraints

1.Robot must be autonomous, no remote control signal whatsoever allowed.
2.Robots must remain on the ground. Jumping, flying, or launching the robot towards the beacon is not allowed. Must be propelled having contact to the ground, via wheels, tracks, or other means.
3.Vehicle must meet one of two size categories.
1.less than 1.5kg (3.31lbs).
2.less than 4.0kg (8.818lbs).
4.Size limits- wheelbase shall be no longer than 20 inches, and height shall be no larger than 30 inches.
5.All hardware costs shall be no more than $500.

Goals

Our plan is to design an autonomous vehicle that will compete in the robotics challenge. The vehicle should be able to navigate towards a source beacon with no human intervention by using various sensors in the vehicle. It should be able to identify and navigate over or around obstacles that are typical of a relatively level but rocky terrain consisting of rocks, small dunes, and crevices. A couple available problems that our plan needs to take into account are large holes that the robot could fall into and ground clearance. Our plan has to account for rocks or other obstacle that the robot could get hung up on and navigate around them. Additionally we plan to keep our robot under 1.5 Kgs.
